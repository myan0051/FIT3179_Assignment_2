What do the costs of living look like?

Details

Here is a breakdown of the average households monthly spending according to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics (2023), Although this data is from the US, Australian and US cities rank very closely on the Cost of Living Index (see map below), therefore it is still representative.
Breakdown:
88% of Monthly income is being spent, leaving 12% for savings.

  • The largest expense is Housing at 33% of monthly spending, a 7% increase from 2022.

  • The second is Healthcare and pensions at 20% of monthly spending, personal insurance and spending had a 11% and healthcare spending had a 7% increase.

  • Transport at 17% of monthly spending costs more than many realize largely due to the intermittent nature of those expenses. It grew 12% from 2022.

  • Food 14% of monthly spending a 13% increase from 2022, the largest increase of all categories

  • Miscellaneous at 16% is listed last because it is an amalgamation of many items, including apparel, personal care products, services, reading and many others.

If we scroll through the Years silder on the bottom left, we can see that relative to New York, global cost of living prices have decreased as the amount of yellow on the map reduces.

In 2023, It seems like the US and Australia are some of the highest cost of living areas with Europe after that and Asia being the lowest.

Some interesting locations are Swizerland, which compared to the surrounding euorpean countries is much more expensive and Hamilton, Bermuda which is the most expensive place to live in the world, likely due to its distance from the nearest trade hub.

What do the costs of living look like?

Details

Here is a breakdown of the average household monthly spending according to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics (2023), Although this data is from the US, Australian and US cities rank very closely on the Cost of Living Index (see map below), therefore it is still representative.
Breakdown:
88% of Monthly income is being spent, leaving 12% for savings.

  • The largest expense is Housing at 33% of monthly spending, a 7% increase from 2022.

  • The second is Healthcare and pensions at 20% of monthly spending, personal insurance had a 11% increase and healthcare spending had a 7% increase.

  • Transport at 17% of monthly spending costs more than many realise largely due to the intermittent nature of those expenses. It grew 12% from 2022.

  • Food 14% at of monthly spending a 13% increase from 2022, the largest increase of all categories

  • Miscellaneous at 16% is listed last because it is an amalgamation of many items, including apparel, personal care products, services, reading and many others.

Compare costs between cities (2023)

Details

This chart allows you to see how much certain items cost in different cities and compare.

The items and quantity is kept constant so the bigger the bubble the more expensive the same item is to buy somewhere in the world.

An interesting comparison is Hamilton, Bermuda (Rank 1) vs Islamabad, Pakistan (Rank 535). The exact same items are ~7.3x more expensive in Hamilton than Islamabad and this is shown in the difference of bubble sizes.

So where should I live?

Scatter plot of Purchasing Power vs Cost of living

Details

We've looked at how expensive / cheap things can be depending on where you live but the place where things are the cheapest isnt necessarily the best place to live.

A good place to live should have cheap cost of living but also a high purchasing power Index rating which measures how much an individual working in that city can afford.

Some notable cities with a good purchasing power to cost of living ratio have been labeled. According to the graph, the best place to live in 2023 is Memphis, United states.
